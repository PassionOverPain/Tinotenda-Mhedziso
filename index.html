About Me

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Information Technology, with a broad proficiency across various IT disciplines. My journey in Information Technology began in high school, where I established a strong foundation in the field. I honed my leadership skills through notable roles such as Head Prefect and President of the Representative Council of learners, experiences that also taught me valuable stakeholder engagement during my tenure on the School Governing Body. At Nelson Mandela University, I further developed my skills as the Events Coordinator of the Computer Society in 2024, focusing on enhancing stakeholder relationships. My soft skills include analytical thinking, effective time management, and team leadership. My coding journey commenced with Delphi, a language with roots as deep as my high school IT teacher's tenure. I progressed to languages such as C# for Windows Applications and MySQL for Database Development. Additionally, I explored Game Development using Unity and C#, crafting basic yet engaging games. I have also delved into Web Development using HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. To me, programming is the process of overcoming a video game boss: seldom do you get it the initial attempts, but through persistence, one levels up and succeeds. I am eager to expand my IT expertise through collaborative projects, hackathons, and potential job opportunities.
